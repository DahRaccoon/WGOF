Keith Flynn: The Reigning King of the Friday Tour

Keith Flynn hoisting the trophy after the 2025 Merkley WGOF Championship.

After a grueling season of "We Golf on Friday" competition, Keith Flynn solidified his legacy by taking home the 2025 WGOF Championship. Known for his ability to scramble out of the Willard woods and drain high-pressure putts, Keith managed to hold off the field in the final major of the year.

Season Analysis | 2025 Recap

The Merkley Paradox: Most Wins, No Trophy

If you look purely at the volume of victories, 2025 belonged to Kyle Merkley. With more match wins and skin captures than any other golfer on the tour, Kyle was the statistical favorite heading into the championship weekend. However, the "Merkley Magic" seemed to hit a wall when it mattered most.

Despite a dominant regular season, Kyle finished as the runner-up in the championship, sparking debates in the WGOF group chat about whether he is the "best player to have never won a November major" in the current era. As we head into 2026, the question remains: Can Kyle translate weekly dominance into a career-defining title?
